Akira Toriyama

The man, The myth, The LEGEND

Japanese manga artist Akira Toriyama in 1982 at the JIJI Press

Here's a time line of Akira Toriyama's life:

  • 1955 - Born in Nagoya, Aichi, Japan on the fifth April

  • 1974 - Starts his career as a manga artist, initially working at a small advertising company

  • 1978 - Makes his debut in the manga world with the series "Wonder Island" published in Weekly Shōnen Jump

  • 1980 - Gains popularity with his successful manga series "Dr. Slump," which wins the Shogakukan Manga Award for best shōnen or shōjo manga series

  • 1984 - Begins his most famous work, "Dragon Ball," which becomes one of the most popular and influential manga series of all time

  • 1986 - "Dragon Ball" is adapted into an anime series, further boosting its popularity and leading to multiple anime adaptations and movies

  • 1995 - Concludes the "Dragon Ball" manga series, but continues to work on related projects and sequels

  • 1996 - Works on "Dragon Ball GT," an anime continuation of the original series (though not directly based on his manga)

  • 2002 - Contributes character designs for the popular video game series "Dragon Quest," becoming a key figure in the franchise

  • 2009 - Oversees the production of "Dragon Ball Kai," a remastered version of the original "Dragon Ball Z" anime

  • 2015 - Involvement in the creation and production of "Dragon Ball Super," a new anime series continuing the story of "Dragon Ball Z"

  • 2021 - Continues to be actively involved in various "Dragon Ball" projects, including manga, anime, and movies

  • 2023 - Celebrates the ongoing success and legacy of "Dragon Ball," which remains a global phenomenon with a dedicated fanbase "Toriyama's creativity and imagination have left an indelible mark on the world of manga and anime, inspiring countless artists and fans around the globe."

  • 2024 - dies at the age of 68.
